Applying Elbers: Total segregation

Alan Engel
Last update: 14 December 2021

Elbers (2021) derives a five-term decomposition: $$S(t_{2})-S(t_{1})=\Delta_{appearing}+\Delta_{disappearing}$$ $$+\Delta_{marginal-units}+\Delta_{marginal-groups}$$ $$+\sum_{u\in t_{1}\cap t_{2}}\Delta_{i,structural}$$ where \(S(t)\) is a segregation index value at time t, \(\Delta_{appearing}\) is the number of units (e.g., occupations) that newly appeared between time \(t_{1}\) and \(t_{2}\), \(\Delta_{disappearing}\) is the number of units that disappeared, \(\Delta_{marginal-units}\) is the marginal change due to units, and \(\Delta_{marginal-groups}\) is that due to groups (for example, genders). The last term is the structural ("pure") segregation that occurs within each unit. When written in this form, the Uchikoshi and Mugiyama (2020) [UM2020] decomposition becomes: $$S(t_{2})-S(t_{1})=\Delta_{marginal-units}+\sum_{u\in t_{1}\cap t_{2}}\Delta_{i,structural}$$

In UM2020 the segregation index is Duncans' Dissimilarity Index. Elbers uses Thiel's H index: $$H=\frac{M}{E(p_{g})}$$ where M is the Mutual Information Index and \(E(p_{g})\) is the group (gender) entropy.

This task follows the analysis that Elbers (2021) provides in its supplementary materials.
